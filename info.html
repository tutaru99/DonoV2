Information

Health requirements

  • A person can individually apply for a donor card at the age of 18 and can be a donor regardless of their age.
  • Minor health issues can`t prevent someone from signing up as a donor. Only dangerous conditions like active cancer or HIV can restrict that.
  • Even if declained for a donation because of their health condition, the donor can still improve it`s health and participate after some time.

What can a person donate

One person can save 8 lives by their vital organs and help 27 others by other donations!

  • Heart, lungs, liver, kidneys, pancreas, even faces and hands.
  • Tissues like skin, bone, cornea, heart valves and connective tissue.
  • Also stem cells and bone marrow

Donating process

For living donors the thought of surgery might be frightening and seem to come with many dangerous consequences,
hovever when it comes to your life it matters the most and being a donor always comes second.

  • The procedure is planned 2-4 weeks ahead so you have time to prepare both mentally and physically.
  • Professional`s priority is to guarantee you safety during the donation process.
  • After the procedure the donor stays at a hospital for up to 1 week to recover.
  • Data about living donor`s recovery process is reported to improve results in the future.

Confidentiality

  • Information of a donor is strictly confidential and will not be revealed, however it is not illegal to share the fact that you are a donor or
    share your experience of deciding to sign up as one.
  • Sharing your story and experience can make a difference and encourage others to sign up. It`s a good deed to help others even after life.
